# Evidence Baseline

Use these anchors as conservative defaults. Do not present them as individualized medical advice.

## Physical Activity Targets

- Adults: 150–300 minutes/week moderate aerobic activity, or 75–150 minutes/week vigorous activity, or an equivalent mix.
- Adults: muscle-strengthening activities for major muscle groups at least 2 days/week.
- Older adults or adults with poor mobility: include multicomponent physical activity emphasizing balance and functional movement.

Sources:

- WHO physical activity fact sheet: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/physical-activity
- WHO BeHealthy physical activity summary: https://www.who.int/initiatives/behealthy/physical-activity

## Resistance Training

ACSM's 2026 resistance-training update emphasizes that moving from no resistance training to consistent resistance training provides major benefit; major muscle groups should be trained regularly, and consistency matters more than a complex “perfect” program for most adults.

Sources:

- ACSM 2026 update summary: https://acsm.org/resistance-training-guidelines-update-2026/
- ACSM physical activity guidelines page: https://acsm.org/education-resources/trending-topics-resources/physical-activity-guidelines/
- ACSM Position Stand article record: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12965823/

## Heart Rate Caveat

Beta-blockers and some cardiovascular medications can blunt the exercise heart-rate response. When HR is unreliable, use RPE, talk test, symptoms, and clinician guidance rather than forcing target HR zones.

Sources:

- American Heart Association beta-blocker overview: https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/consumer-healthcare/medication-information/how-do-beta-blocker-drugs-affect-exercise
- Mayo Clinic beta-blockers and exercise: https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/high-blood-pressure/expert-answers/beta-blockers/faq-20058369

## Aging, Strength, Bone, and Function

Strength training supports healthier aging and physical function. Resistance training, balance, and weight-bearing activity are relevant to bone integrity, mobility, and fall-risk reduction. For diagnosed osteoporosis, recent fracture, or high fracture risk, stay conservative and recommend clinician/physio guidance.
