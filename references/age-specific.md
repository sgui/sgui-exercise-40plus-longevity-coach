# Age-Specific Rules for 40+ Training

Use this for deeper handling of 40+ issues after the core 40+ checklist fires: menopause/perimenopause, sarcopenia, tendon/joint ramping, bone-density emphasis, return-after-break, 60+, low baseline, balance, or fall risk.

## 40+ Differentiators

Do not merely make a generic plan easier. Account for:

- **Sarcopenia prevention**: preserve or build muscle through regular resistance training, major movement patterns, and progressive overload.
- **Tendon and joint tolerance**: progress load, impact, range, and volume gradually; avoid sudden spikes after breaks.
- **Bone density**: include resistance training, weight-bearing cardio when tolerated, balance, and fall-prevention work. Do not avoid all loading by default; scale it.
- **Balance and power**: include low-risk balance and controlled power exposure when appropriate, especially for older/less active users.
- **Recovery variability**: sleep, stress, soreness, and hard sport sessions should change the day’s dose.
- **Perimenopause/menopause**: consider strength, bone, muscle, sleep, heat tolerance, fatigue, and symptom variability. Do not give hormone or medical advice.
- **Cardiometabolic health**: combine strength with moderate cardio / Zone 2 or talk-test-based aerobic work.

## Age-Aware Defaults

- Start with the lowest effective volume and progress after consistency is stable.
- Keep 1–3 reps in reserve for most strength work unless the user is experienced and ready.
- Use 24–48 hour recoverability as the main quality check.
- Favor compound patterns, but choose joint-friendly variations.
- Add balance 2–5 times/week in small doses: tandem stance, single-leg stance near support, carries, step-ups, controlled direction changes.
- For bone: use progressive resistance and weight-bearing activity if tolerated; avoid high-impact jumps if pain, osteoporosis risk, poor balance, or no training base.

## Menopause / Perimenopause Handling

When the user is female 40+ or mentions menopause/perimenopause symptoms:

- Ask about symptom constraints only if relevant: sleep disruption, hot flashes, joint pain, fatigue, cycle variability, pelvic-floor considerations, or clinician restrictions.
- Prioritize strength training, bone-supportive loading, balance, and recoverability.
- Use RPE and performance trend rather than assuming every bad session is hormonal.
- Avoid medical claims about hormone therapy, supplements, or disease treatment.

## Return-After-Break Handling

If the user has had a break of 2+ weeks:

- First week: Yellow by default unless clearly trained and symptom-free.
- Use 60–80% of previous volume or load.
- Avoid introducing new high-impact or high-complexity movements in the same week.
- Require one recoverable repeat before progressing.

## Older Adult / Low Baseline Handling

For 60+, low baseline, fall concern, or poor mobility:

- Start with chair-supported, wall-supported, or rail-supported variations.
- Include sit-to-stand, hip hinge practice, step-up, carry, row/pull, push, and balance.
- Avoid floor exercises if getting up/down is unsafe or discouraging; offer alternatives.
- Keep instructions simple and executable.
