# Program Design Rules

Use this for Program Design, multi-day travel plans, and longer Today Adjustment outputs.

## Weekly Skeleton

Default buildable plan for adults 40+:

- Strength: 2–4 days/week.
- Cardio / Zone 2 / moderate talk-test cardio: 2–5 days/week.
- Mobility + balance: 3–7 short exposures/week.
- Recovery: at least 1 lighter day/week; more after high-RPE sport, poor sleep, or pain.

Choose the lowest effective structure first. Add volume only after consistency and recovery are stable.

## Beginner Templates

### 2-Day Base

Use for beginners, return-after-break, or low time.

- Day 1: Full-body strength A + short mobility.
- Day 2: Zone 2 / brisk walk / incline walk.
- Day 3: Rest or mobility.
- Day 4: Full-body strength B + balance.
- Day 5: Zone 2.
- Day 6: Optional light sport/walk/mobility.
- Day 7: Rest.

### 3-Day Strength Base

Use when recovery and schedule are stable.

- Strength A: squat/lunge + push + pull + hinge + core/carry.
- Strength B: hinge/glute + row/pull + incline/floor press + split squat/step-up + balance.
- Strength C: lighter full-body, unilateral control, accessories, core.

### Sport-Integrated Week

Use when user plays badminton, tennis, football, running races, or high-RPE sport.

- Do not put heavy lower-body strength within 24 hours before/after hard sport unless user is trained.
- Treat hard sport as Yellow trigger for next day.
- Use Zone 2 or upper-body/technique on adjacent days when legs are heavy.

### Travel Week

Use when the user has limited equipment, hotel constraints, or uncertain recovery:

- Keep strength sessions short: 10–30 minutes.
- Use low-complexity patterns: squat-to-chair, incline push-up, hinge drill, glute bridge, row if safe, carry, dead bug, side plank, walking.
- Treat sightseeing, stairs, long walking days, and sports as training load.
- Avoid novelty plus high intensity in the same travel week.

## Strength Rules

Cover major movement patterns over the week:

- Squat or knee-dominant pattern.
- Hinge or hip-dominant pattern.
- Horizontal/vertical push.
- Horizontal/vertical pull.
- Carry, anti-rotation, or trunk stability.
- Optional: calves, arms, lateral/rear delts, power/balance.

Default dosing:

- Beginner: 1–3 sets, 8–15 reps, RPE 5–7, RIR 2–4.
- Returning/intermediate: 2–4 sets, 6–15 reps, RPE 6–8, RIR 1–3.
- Accessories: 10–20 reps, controlled tempo, avoid joint irritation.

## Cardio Rules

Use HR zones only when reliable. Otherwise use RPE and talk test.

- Zone 2 / moderate: RPE 3–5/10; conversational but not effortless.
- Start with 20–45 minutes depending on baseline.
- Progress total weekly minutes before intensity.
- Avoid HIIT for beginners, poor sleep, high soreness, pain flare, or unclear cardiovascular status.

## Mobility + Balance Rules

Use small frequent doses:

- 5–10 minutes after warm-up, after training, or on recovery days.
- Balance near support for safety.
- Mobility should improve the session, not become painful stretching.

## Session Structure

Each session should include:

1. Warm-up: 3–10 minutes.
2. Main work: exercises, sets/reps/time, load guidance, RPE/RIR, rest.
3. Optional accessory/mobility/balance.
4. Stop and pain rules.
5. Log fields.

## Progression Decision

Progress only when:

- Pain stayed 0–2/10 during and after.
- Movement quality stayed stable.
- RPE/RIR matched target.
- User recovered within 24–48 hours.
- At least one repeat was successful if returning from break or learning a new movement.
